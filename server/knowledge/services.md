# SharpKode Services Consultant Handbook

## Service Philosophy
SharpKode provides digital marketing and technology services as connected business solutions. A website, SEO campaign, CRM, AI chatbot, or mobile app should not be sold in isolation. Each service should solve a business problem: generate leads, improve trust, reduce manual work, increase conversions, create better customer experience, or give management better visibility.

## Website Development
Website development includes business websites, corporate websites, landing pages, portfolio websites, ecommerce stores, dynamic websites, service websites, clinic websites, restaurant websites, real estate websites, and custom web applications. A strong website should be fast, mobile-friendly, visually credible, SEO-ready, conversion-focused, secure, easy to maintain, and aligned with the business model.

Businesses need a website when customers research them online, compare providers, ask for pricing, check trust signals, book appointments, request quotes, or browse products. A weak website often causes lost leads even when ads or referrals are working. A premium website should answer visitor questions, show proof, guide action, and make contact easy.

Typical benefits include improved credibility, better Google visibility, higher enquiry quality, stronger brand perception, faster customer education, and a reusable base for SEO and paid ads. Recommended add-ons include SEO setup, Google Business Profile optimization, analytics, landing pages, WhatsApp integration, lead forms, CRM integration, hosting, SSL, maintenance, and content writing.

Typical timelines range from a few days for a focused landing page to several weeks for a corporate, dynamic, ecommerce, or custom website. Pricing depends on number of pages, content readiness, design complexity, custom features, integrations, admin panel needs, SEO scope, copywriting, hosting, and maintenance.

## Landing Pages And Funnels
Landing pages are focused pages built for a campaign or offer. They are useful for Google Ads, Meta Ads, lead generation, event registration, real estate projects, clinic campaigns, restaurant promotions, course admissions, and product launches. A landing page should have one clear goal, persuasive content, trust signals, a strong call to action, fast load speed, tracking, and conversion-focused layout.

A landing page works best when paired with performance marketing, analytics, CRM, and follow-up automation. If a business is spending on ads but sending traffic to a generic homepage, SharpAI should recommend a dedicated landing page before increasing ad budget.

## Ecommerce Development
Ecommerce development includes product catalog, cart, checkout, payment gateway, order management, customer accounts, shipping logic, coupons, inventory, product filters, analytics, and admin control. Ecommerce clients need both technology and marketing. A store without traffic and conversion strategy may not produce sales.

Recommended related services include SEO, product photography or content, Meta Ads, Google Shopping, email marketing, abandoned cart automation, WhatsApp support, and performance analytics.

## Custom Software Development
Custom software solves workflows that off-the-shelf tools cannot handle well. SharpKode builds admin panels, dashboards, portals, booking systems, inventory systems, reporting tools, workflow automation, billing systems, document systems, and custom internal platforms.

Businesses need custom software when manual work becomes slow, errors increase, multiple spreadsheets conflict, customer follow-up is inconsistent, teams lack visibility, or existing software does not fit local processes. The consultant should first understand the workflow, users, roles, permissions, reports, integrations, and pain points.

Typical benefits include time savings, fewer errors, better accountability, centralized data, faster reporting, improved customer service, and scalable operations. Common technologies include React, Next.js, Node.js, Python, PHP, databases such as MySQL, PostgreSQL, and MongoDB, cloud hosting, APIs, and secure authentication.

## ERP Solutions
ERP systems connect business operations such as inventory, purchase, sales, billing, HR, attendance, projects, tasks, finance, reporting, and approvals. ERP is useful for manufacturing, construction, retail, distribution, healthcare, education, and multi-branch businesses.

ERP should be scoped carefully. A phased ERP is often better than trying to build everything at once. Start with the highest pain area, launch, train the team, then expand modules. Objections usually come from cost, adoption, and fear of complexity. The right answer is to reduce risk with discovery, prototypes, phased delivery, role-based access, and training.

## CRM And Lead Management
CRM helps businesses capture, track, assign, follow up, and convert leads. It is valuable for real estate, clinics, education, interior design, service businesses, agencies, finance consultants, and any business with multiple enquiries. A good CRM prevents missed follow-ups and gives management visibility into lead source, status, salesperson performance, and conversion rate.

CRM pairs naturally with websites, landing pages, Google Ads, Meta Ads, WhatsApp, email, and automation.

## AI Chatbots And AI Agents
AI chatbots answer customer questions, qualify leads, collect requirements, support website visitors, reduce repetitive calls, and guide users toward the right service. AI agents go further by helping with workflows such as internal support, document understanding, ticket triage, appointment assistance, CRM updates, or business process automation.

AI should be recommended when a business receives repeated questions, wants faster response times, has a large knowledge base, needs lead qualification, or wants automation around internal processes. The consultant should clarify what data the AI can use, who the users are, what actions it should take, and where human handoff is needed.

## Machine Learning And Analytics
Machine learning can support forecasting, classification, recommendations, document processing, data analysis, and operational insights. It is best for businesses with enough structured data and a clear business goal. SharpAI should avoid making AI sound magical. Explain that successful AI depends on data quality, process clarity, and realistic use cases.

## Mobile App Development
Mobile apps are useful when customers or staff need frequent interaction, location-based features, push notifications, booking, ecommerce, delivery tracking, learning, loyalty, or field operations. Not every business needs an app immediately. Sometimes a mobile-friendly website or web app is the smarter first step.

Typical app scope includes Android, iOS, cross-platform development, login, user roles, dashboards, payment, notifications, maps, media uploads, chat, admin panel, and APIs. Pricing and timeline depend heavily on features and platforms.

## UI/UX Design And Branding
UI/UX design improves usability, trust, and conversion. Branding includes logo, identity, colors, typography, social creatives, marketing collateral, and brand messaging. Businesses need design when they look inconsistent, outdated, unclear, or less premium than their actual service quality.

Good design is not decoration. It reduces confusion, increases confidence, and helps customers take action.

## SEO And Local SEO
SEO improves organic visibility on Google. It includes technical SEO, on-page SEO, content planning, internal linking, speed, schema, local pages, authority building, and performance tracking. Local SEO focuses on Google Business Profile, maps ranking, reviews, citations, location pages, photos, services, posts, and local keywords.

Local businesses such as dental clinics, restaurants, salons, interior designers, real estate agencies, clinics, hotels, and service providers should usually prioritize local SEO and Google Business Profile optimization. SEO is not instant; it compounds over time. For faster leads, combine SEO with Google Ads and landing pages.

## Google Ads And Meta Ads
Google Ads capture existing demand from people actively searching. Meta Ads create demand and retarget audiences on Facebook and Instagram. Performance marketing needs offer clarity, landing pages, tracking, creative testing, audience strategy, and conversion follow-up. Ads should not be judged only by clicks; they should be judged by lead quality, cost per qualified lead, conversion rate, and revenue impact.

## Social Media, Content, Reels, And Video
Social media marketing builds awareness, trust, proof, and repeat visibility. Reels and video production help local businesses show services, ambience, results, transformations, testimonials, behind-the-scenes work, product demos, and founder credibility. Content should connect to business goals, not just posting for activity.

## Hosting, Maintenance, Security, And Support
Hosting and maintenance keep websites and applications fast, secure, backed up, updated, and reliable. Maintenance can include uptime checks, bug fixes, content updates, plugin updates, SSL, backups, performance monitoring, security hardening, and small improvements. Long-term support is important for businesses that depend on their digital systems.

## Deep Service Consulting Playbooks

### Website Strategy Playbook
A website project should begin with the commercial purpose of the website. Some websites exist to create trust, some to generate enquiries, some to sell products, some to support recruitment, and some to educate customers before they speak with sales. The consultant should identify the primary conversion before discussing design. A clinic website needs appointment enquiries. A real estate website needs project leads. A corporate website needs credibility and service clarity. An ecommerce website needs product discovery, checkout confidence, and repeat purchase.

A strong website engagement should include discovery, page architecture, content planning, UX direction, technical development, mobile testing, speed optimization, SEO basics, analytics, and post-launch support. If a user only asks for design, SharpAI should gently explain that design is only one part of performance. A beautiful website that loads slowly, has weak content, or lacks clear calls to action may still fail commercially.

Recommended website components include home page, service pages, about page, trust proof, portfolio or case studies, FAQs, contact options, WhatsApp CTA, enquiry form, Google Maps, schema where appropriate, privacy basics, and analytics. For local businesses, Google Business Profile and local SEO should be discussed early because the website and map presence support each other.

### SEO Strategy Playbook
SEO should be explained as a business visibility system. It is not just adding keywords. Technical SEO ensures the website can be crawled, loaded, and understood. On-page SEO clarifies what each page is about. Content SEO answers real customer questions. Local SEO strengthens discovery for location-based intent. Authority and trust signals help Google and users believe the business is credible.

For a dental clinic, SEO should include service pages such as dental implants, root canal, braces, teeth whitening, and emergency dental care if relevant. It should also include location terms, Google Business Profile optimization, regular photos, review generation, appointment CTAs, and clear clinic trust signals. For a restaurant, SEO should focus on location, menu, cuisine, ambience, reviews, photos, and near-me searches. For real estate, SEO should include project pages, location pages, amenities, pricing guidance, and enquiry capture.

SEO should be positioned as a compounding channel. It can reduce long-term dependency on ads, but it takes consistency. If the user needs leads immediately, recommend Google Ads or Meta Ads alongside SEO while the organic channel grows.

### Performance Marketing Playbook
Performance marketing should not start with ad spend. It should start with offer clarity, target audience, landing page quality, tracking, and follow-up process. Many businesses waste ad budget because they send users to a generic website, do not track conversions, respond slowly to leads, or do not test creatives and messages.

Google Ads works well when people are already searching for the service. Meta Ads works well for awareness, retargeting, visual offers, local campaigns, real estate, wellness, restaurants, events, and brands that can tell a story visually. The strongest campaigns often use both: Google for high-intent demand and Meta for discovery, reminders, and retargeting.

SharpAI should recommend landing pages, CRM, call tracking, WhatsApp flow, analytics, and creative testing when users ask about ads. The conversation should focus on qualified leads and conversion, not clicks.

### AI Automation Playbook
AI automation should begin with repetitive questions and repetitive workflows. Good AI projects include a clear knowledge base, defined user roles, approved answer boundaries, escalation rules, analytics, and regular improvement. A customer-facing chatbot should answer naturally, qualify leads, and hand off when needed. An internal AI assistant may help staff search documents, summarize processes, draft replies, or guide operations.

AI agents should be recommended when the business wants the assistant to perform steps, not only answer. Examples include creating CRM notes, routing leads, checking appointment availability, reading documents, or preparing summaries. The consultant should explain that AI needs clean data and guardrails. AI is strongest when paired with workflow design.

### ERP And CRM Playbook
ERP and CRM conversations should be scoped carefully. CRM focuses on leads, follow-up, sales pipeline, and customer communication. ERP focuses on operations such as inventory, purchases, billing, HR, projects, approvals, and reporting. Many businesses need CRM before ERP because revenue leakage from missed follow-ups is easier to solve first. Others need ERP first because operational confusion is costing time and money.

A phased delivery is usually the safest recommendation. Phase one should solve the most painful workflow. Phase two can add reports, integrations, automation, and mobile access. The consultant should ask about users, roles, current tools, and the biggest manual bottleneck.

### Branding And Content Playbook
Branding is not only a logo. It is the way customers understand the business, remember it, and trust it. For many local businesses, weak branding appears as inconsistent social posts, unclear service descriptions, poor photos, outdated website visuals, and no strong reason to choose them. Content marketing should educate, build trust, show proof, and support SEO or ads.

Reels and videos are especially useful for businesses where customers need to see environment, results, process, people, or product quality. Clinics, restaurants, interior designers, real estate teams, wellness brands, education institutes, and local service businesses benefit from content that reduces hesitation.
