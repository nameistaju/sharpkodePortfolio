# SharpKode Process Consultant Handbook

## Discovery First
Every strong project begins with discovery. The team should understand the business, audience, current challenges, goals, competitors, existing assets, budget range, timeline, and decision process. SharpAI should encourage discovery before proposing complex systems.

## Requirement Gathering
Requirement gathering turns vague ideas into clear scope. For websites, gather page list, content, references, CTA, SEO targets, integrations, and design preferences. For software, gather users, roles, workflow steps, reports, permissions, data, integrations, and edge cases. For marketing, gather goals, target geography, audience, current channels, offer, monthly budget, and tracking.

## Strategy And Planning
Planning should define the recommended solution, phases, timeline, responsibilities, technology approach, content needs, and success metrics. A smaller clear scope is better than a large unclear scope.

## Design
Design includes wireframes, UI direction, user journey, brand consistency, responsive layouts, and conversion thinking. Design should serve business goals. A website design should guide visitors toward trust and action. A software design should make daily work easier.

## Development
Development should follow the approved scope and architecture. Good development includes clean code, reusable components, secure forms, validations, responsive layouts, database design, APIs, admin panels, integrations, and testing.

## Content And SEO Setup
Content should answer customer questions, explain services, build trust, and support search visibility. SEO setup may include titles, meta descriptions, headings, schema, internal links, speed, mobile performance, and Google tools.

## Testing
Testing should check responsiveness, forms, links, speed, browser compatibility, validation, payment flow if any, admin panel, security basics, and user workflows. For software, user acceptance testing is important.

## Deployment
Deployment includes hosting setup, SSL, domain/DNS, environment variables, database, backups, security settings, and launch checks. The launch should be planned rather than rushed.

## Training
For software, ERP, CRM, and admin panels, training helps teams adopt the system. Documentation or walkthroughs reduce support friction.

## Support And Improvement
After launch, real users reveal improvements. Maintenance, analytics, SEO, campaign optimization, content updates, and software enhancements create long-term value. SharpKode should position projects as evolving business assets.

## Phased Delivery
For complex projects, phased delivery reduces risk. Phase one may include core website, MVP software, CRM basics, or priority ERP module. Later phases can add automation, analytics, integrations, mobile app, or marketing expansion.

## Discovery Workshop Detail
A discovery workshop should uncover business model, audience, current digital assets, competitors, operational workflow, decision makers, must-have features, nice-to-have features, risk areas, and success metrics. For a website, the workshop may focus on brand, pages, content, SEO, and conversion. For software, it may map current process, user roles, data, reports, and integrations. For marketing, it may map target geography, offer, sales process, ad budget, and tracking.

## Proposal And Scope Discipline
A good proposal should define deliverables, assumptions, exclusions, timeline, payment stages, responsibilities, and support terms. Scope discipline protects both client and team. If new ideas appear during development, they should be handled as phase two or change requests rather than silently expanding the project.

## Launch And Growth
Launch is not the end. After launch, review analytics, enquiries, search performance, page behavior, campaign performance, and user feedback. For websites, this may lead to new landing pages or SEO content. For software, this may lead to workflow improvements. For marketing, this may lead to creative testing and budget reallocation.
